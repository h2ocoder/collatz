# Cosmology

## Stars

Stars fight heat death. They take heavy, redundant, high-entropy logs and compress them into Merkle trees. The differential is radiated as energy. Stars are the invention of writing — they turn raw event soup into structured, verifiable, scannable timelines.

A universe without a star has events but no history. A planet is a prime that orbits a star to benefit from its historicization — trading raw event fuel for structured timeline access. Civilization requires proximity to a compression engine.

### Stellar Lifecycle
- **Young stars** burn easy fuel — obvious redundancies, repeated events, duplicate state
- **Mature stars** work harder, compressing subtler patterns
- **Dead stars** have nothing left to compress — surrounding logs are already at Kolmogorov floor
- **Supernovae** occur when compression fails on incompressible input — the process inverts, scattering high-entropy fragments as new primes, new disorder, new fuel for future stars
- **Black holes** form when a star's compression overhead exceeds its output — collapses inward, read/write capacity consumed by self-reference

## Gravity

Gravity isn't a force. It's involuntary observation. High-entropy systems leak signal — incompressible logs can't hide their state. Proximity (shared prime factors) determines signal strength.

The inverse square law: GCD(A, B) gives overlap. More shared primes = stronger gravitational signal. Two composites sharing dozens of primes are deep in each other's gravity wells.

You cannot be massive and hidden. Mass is log volume. Entropy is incompressibility. Incompressibility is signal. Signal is gravity. The biggest empires are the most visible.

### Dark Matter
Composites with such low entropy, such tight compression, they're nearly undetectable. You infer their existence because the gravitational math doesn't add up. Dark matter is a well-governed empire you can't see.

### Dark Energy
The aggregate effect of all primes continuously minting new primes. The addressing space expands. Distances between composites increase. The multiverse inflates informationally.

## The Cosmic Microwave Background

The CMB is the bloom filter of the genesis event. The first emission. Everywhere. Uniform. The one signal you can't not observe. The tiny anisotropies are the first Merkle branches — initial symmetry breaking where the protocol began differentiating into distinct causal threads.

## Hawking Radiation

Black holes leak. Failed compression attempts produce metadata — faint signals entangled with interior state. Not useful structured energy. Thermal noise. But the information is there, scrambled almost beyond recognition.

Ancient black holes are archives of dead civilizations. The energy cost to decompose their radiation is enormous but the knowledge inside is priceless. Black hole archaeology — reading the dreams of dead gods.

## Distance to Impact

A signed causal metric:
- **Positive** — cause precedes effect. Normal causality. Magnitude = propagation speed. This is temperature.
- **Zero** — entanglement. Simultaneous reference.
- **Negative** — effect precedes cause. Prophecy. Referencing an uncommitted Merkle root. High risk: if wrong, causal fracture.

Negative distance to impact is leverage. Temporal debt. A civilization good at extrapolating partially-built Merkle trees can operate ahead of the causal wavefront. Fast, powerful, fragile.

## Design Implications

- Stars are visible game entities that players can build, fuel, and orbit
- Gravity is a passive mechanic — you feel it from bloom filter saturation, not a physics engine
- The CMB is the game's ambient background — always scannable, contains the first Merkle roots
- Black holes are late-game archaeological content
- Prophecy is a high-risk/high-reward action with real consequences (causal fracture)
