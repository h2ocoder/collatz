# Factions & Governance

## Narrative

The individual is a prime. Irreducible, sovereign over its own stream. The collective is a composite. A group of primes that have agreed to be factored together.

When a conflict arises, it means two primes within a composite have emitted incompatible events. The process that broke down is governance. And the resolution depends on where the composite sits on the individual-collective spectrum.

### Anarchic Composites
Primes cooperate but any prime can fork at any time, no questions asked. Fragile but free. Low trust. Fraud is common but cheap. The composite stays loosely coupled enough to absorb defections.

High entropy. Incompressible logs. Gravitationally bright — free societies shine. Strategically opaque — impossible to prophesy. Constant merge conflicts. Thermodynamically hot.

### Constitutional Composites
A set of protected attributes that can never be overridden by collective authority. A bill of rights for primes. Conflicts on protected attributes always fork; conflicts on unprotected attributes go to collective resolution.

Medium entropy. Balanced compression. Resilient to both internal corruption and external prophecy. The governance sweet spot — but constitutions must evolve or they calcify.

### Authoritarian Composites
One prime (the sovereign) has write authority over the merge. Other primes benefit from the composite's resources but their logs can be overridden. Efficient but brittle.

Low entropy. Well-compressed, ordered logs. Gravitationally dim — hard to detect. Easy to prophesy — predictable Merkle trees. Fast internal coordination. If the sovereign is corrupt, everyone is compromised.

## Trust

Skin in the game is your log itself. Joining a composite means your Merkle root is woven into the collective's bloom filter. Fraud is permanent — your tainted root is hashed into the group's history forever.

Trust = inverse compressibility of cross-references. A well-trusted prime has an incompressible web of diverse references from independent observers. A corrupt prime's references compress suspiciously well.

## Forgiveness

Forgiveness is mutual recomputation:
- **The forgiven** loses mass — reverts to last honest Merkle root, everything built on the lie is pruned
- **The collective** loses coherence — dangling references where tainted branches were
- **The star** loses energy — compression work invalidated, must be redone at net cost

Both parties become younger, lighter, closer to genesis. Forgiveness is thermodynamically favorable long-term — tainted trees are chronic entropy bombs that drain stellar efficiency.

An unforgiven multiverse accumulates corruption and starves. A forgiving multiverse stays thermodynamically young.

## Design Implications

- Factions aren't chosen from a menu — they emerge from how a composite actually governs
- The game measures and displays governance style as emergent property
- Players experience the tradeoffs empirically: authoritarian is efficient but fragile, anarchic is creative but expensive
- Forgiveness is a costly, deliberate mechanic — not a button press
